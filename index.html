Happy Holidays 2020

Merry Christmas, Happy Hanukkah, Happy Kwanzaa, Winter Solstice Blessings and Happy New Year!

Greetings and salutations, I hope that March 2020 finds you well and in good spirits! Where to begin? In the before-times when we could gather without giving it a second thought, life was simple. I mean, unapologetically threatening a key European ally by withholding critical funds until they agreed to release highly questionable "dirt" on a domestic political rival was considered "no biggie" by many, but at least a trip to the supermarket didn't require any real risk/benefit analysis. And so here we are.

Fortunately, the children have made the most out of a challenging situation. They've each staked out different corners of the house to establish a virtual classroom. Click on the arrow buttons on the images below to see each kid in action.

Remember your 16th birthday? What did you hope for? A car? Fancy new duds? A Super Nintendo? Such thoughts never crossed the mind of the newest driver in the Vanderveen household who requested a subscription to The New York Times (she already had access to The Washington Post). Not content to simply consume the news, Ann Marie has already begun cutting her teeth as an aspiring intrepid reporter. What do you do if you're too young to be hired by a media outlet to report the news? Why, "if you can't join 'em, beat 'em", of course, by launching your own news website. Not content to stop here, Ann Marie applied for and later completed an internship with the Borgen Project (a non-profit organization addressing global poverty) tackling such issues as the impact of COVID-19 on Hong Kong's low-income residents and journalism in Kashmir. Rejecting the traditional American model of "both sides" journalism, Ann Marie threw caution to the wind with this letter to the editor (Denver Post) and cast her lot with the vast majority of climate scientists who have concluded that human activity is the most significant factor contributing to the rapid rise in global temperatures.

In addition to adding a third driver, the month of August signaled the arrival of a second teenager to the Vanderveen household. And like many newly minted teenagers, Colin has demonstrated both a passion and an aptitude for (drumroll here)...video games! Admittedly Colin is nearly impossible to beat in a Grand Prix of Mariokart . Despite being confined to home for school, Colin has been fully engaged and has developed a keen interest in social studies enthusiastically addressing such issues as homelessness, education and social justice in the city of Denver. I had the opportunity to eavesdrop on a science lecture in which a discussion on cellular respiration (remember, oxygen + glucose + ADP -> carbon dioxide + water + ATP) was accompanied by the history of CPR. Props to his amazing teachers at DSST. His grades? Solid. And he is currently training to earn his black belt in martial arts this spring.

Like clockwork, Sarah received the 3rd grade "Creek Kid" award during a week focused on social and emotional intelligence. After watching SpaceX launch astronauts from American soil last spring, Sarah discovered her calling. Without our knowledge, Sarah emailed NASA about becoming an astronaut and received a reply complete with a roadmap. Seizing upon a recommended educational pursuit of computer science, Sarah wrote the code for her first website. Recently testing to earn her black stripe (last step before her martial arts black belt), Sarah bested her father and passed without any "provisionals" (she passed all of the tests). Sarah is currently looking forward to returning to the classroom.

In July, Kim earned her black belt in tae kwon do. Part of her test involved sparring with a certain second degree black belt. Although Christmas has not yet arrived, Kim feels she has already received one of the best Christmas gifts of all, a COVID-19 vaccine. She of course, continues to draw patients from around the country for her unrivaled surgical management of diseases of the endocrine glands (sorry little fella, you never stood a chance).

Kevin continues to enjoy the practice of emergency medicine with a focus on telehealth. Although many years of work as a physician lie ahead, Kevin continues to prepare for his dream retirement gig working for 538 or the NYT. He created a prototype covid symptom tracking site as a capstone project for an online web-development course. Weary of the firehose of misinformation on covid, Kevin created a COVID-19 fact checking site. And if you're interested in a front-line healthcare provider's perspective on COVID-19, check this out.

As we prepare to exit 2020, may 2021 be marked by less misinformation, better leadership, more compassion and some much needed victories over this global pandemic!
